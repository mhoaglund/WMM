General Layout

CSS is your toolbox for creating structure within your webpage, beyond what HTML alone is capable of. CSS is also the source of the webpage's aesthetic- design of the layout, colors, typefaces, and so forth.

This is a floated div on the left side.
This is a floated div on the right side.
Notice how floated divs don't automatically fill any space-
they scale to their contents, in this case text.
Floats can be used to align elements to the left side (which is a default) or the right side of their parent container. However, they are mostly useful for making text flow around things- like an inset photograph in an article. Don't base your layout on floats! They are really unwieldy compared to more modern stuff like flex-box.
